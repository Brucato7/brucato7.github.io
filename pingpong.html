Ping-Pong, not a way of life, it's a Game

Why Ping-Pong is the Greatest

Ping-Pong is a game's game just as Jon is a man's man. The kind of game another game could cuddle up at night to. Dependable, enjoyable, and easy to please. Who could get angry at the sound of celluloid balls bouncing off of the wooden tables and padded paddles? Only the worst the human race has to offer. It inspired the first popular video game "Pong"! Only such a gentlemanly and noble game could.

Ping-Pong Hero

One of the best ping-pong players ever was Belgian player Jean-Michel Saive. (Only one of the best because we all know that ping-pong players of a different descent tend to be the best... not to be racist...) Jean-Michel Saive played professionally for 34 years. He was the top ping-pong player in Belgium from 1985-2011, uninterrupted. Saive is most widely known internationally for his love of the game and his sportmanship. Check out this video of his match against good friend Chuang Chih-Yuan at the Tai-Ben Invitational tournament in 2014.
